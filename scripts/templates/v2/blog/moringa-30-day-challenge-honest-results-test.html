I Took Moringa Every Day for 30 Days: My Honest Results

Home › Blog › I Took Moringa Every Day for 30 Days

Look, I'll be honest with you. When my friend first mentioned moringa powder, I thought she was trying to sell me another one of those wellness pyramid scheme products. You know the type. Green powder that costs more than my grocery bill and promises to basically turn you into a superhuman.

But she wasn't selling anything. She just looked... different. More awake. Less of that tired, dragging-through-Thursday energy that all of us seem to accept as normal after thirty.

So I did what any skeptical person does. I googled it. Then I fell down a research rabbit hole at midnight. Then I ordered a bag of organic moringa powder at 2am, slightly delirious and very hopeful.

That was thirty-one days ago. Here's everything that happened — the good, the annoying, and the stuff no wellness influencer bothers to mention.

What Even Is Moringa? (The Quick Version)

Before we dive into my experience, let me give you the basics without all the superfood marketing nonsense.

Moringa oleifera comes from a tree that grows in parts of India, Africa, and Southeast Asia. People have been using it for thousands of years, which honestly means more to me than any modern study. If something's been around that long, there's probably something to it.

The leaves get dried and ground into a fine green powder. It's loaded with vitamins A, C, and E, has more iron than spinach, more calcium than milk, and contains all nine essential amino acids. On paper, it looks impressive. But paper promises and real-life results are very different things.

Moringa by the Numbers

  • Vitamin A: 4x more than carrots
  • Vitamin C: 7x more than oranges
  • Calcium: 4x more than milk
  • Iron: 3x more than spinach
  • Protein: 2x more than yoghurt
  • All 9 essential amino acids: Yes — rare for a plant source

The taste? Well. Let's just say it's an acquired one. Imagine if grass clippings and matcha had a baby, but that baby was slightly bitter and very, very green. More on that in the recipes section.

Days 1–7: The "What Have I Done" Phase

I started with one teaspoon in my morning smoothie, like every blog recommended. First mistake.

The smoothie turned an aggressive shade of green that looked like something a cartoon villain would drink. The taste was... fine? Not good, but not terrible. Just very, very earthy. My partner took one look at it and said "that looks like pond water." Thanks, babe.

Day 1–2

Felt nothing. Obviously. Day two, still nothing — but I did spend twenty minutes researching whether I'd bought fake moringa powder because surely something this hyped should do something immediately.

Day 3–4

Ready to quit. Not because I felt bad, but because I felt exactly the same. My morning energy was still terrible. I still needed three cups of coffee to function. The only difference was my smoothie tasted weird.

Day 5

Things shifted. Subtly. So subtly I almost missed it. I realised at 11am that I hadn't thought about coffee yet. I'm someone who usually starts fantasising about my second cup around 9:30am. Not a huge energy surge — just steady. Like my energy wasn't doing its usual rollercoaster thing.

Important: Don't judge moringa by week one. If you're feeling nothing, that's completely normal. Real benefits for most people appear after day seven at the earliest. Stick with it.

Days 8–14: Okay, Something's Happening Here

By the end of week two, I couldn't deny it anymore. Something was genuinely different.

My 3pm energy crash basically disappeared. That thing where you're sitting at your desk and your eyelids feel like they weigh five pounds each? Gone. Not replaced with jittery caffeine energy. Just consistent, calm alertness.

My digestion improved too, which I wasn't expecting. I've dealt with bloating and irregular bathroom situations for years. Thought it was just how my body worked. But by day fourteen, things were smoother. More regular. I know it's not glamorous to talk about, but if you've struggled with this, you know how life-changing it is.

The weird part? My skin started looking different. Clearer, somehow. My partner even asked if I'd started using a new face cream.

Week 2 Changes I Noticed

  • Afternoon energy crash: gone
  • Digestion: noticeably smoother
  • Skin: subtle but real improvement in clarity
  • Morning alertness: better without extra coffee

Here's what I did during this phase because the straight powder was still rough: I mixed one teaspoon of moringa with half a banana, a handful of spinach (ironic, I know), frozen mango, almond milk, and a tiny bit of honey. The sweetness from the mango and banana completely masked that grassy taste. Game changer. Full recipes are below.

Days 15–22: The Adjustment Period

Nothing's perfect, and moringa powder is no exception.

Around day seventeen, I noticed I was feeling slightly nauseous in the mornings. Did some research and realised I'd been taking it on an empty stomach. Apparently moringa is pretty potent, and some people need food with it.

I adjusted. Started having it with breakfast instead of before. Problem solved within two days.

This is the stuff nobody talks about in glowing superfood reviews. The adjustment period. The trial and error. The fact that what works for someone else might not work exactly the same for you. If you feel off in week three — check your timing and whether you're eating with it.

I also noticed that taking moringa too late in the day affected my sleep. Not in a bad way, exactly — more like my brain stayed alert longer than usual. So I moved my dose to morning only. Much better.

Week 3 Adjustments That Fixed Things

  • Nausea fix: Take with food, not on an empty stomach
  • Sleep fix: Morning only, not afternoon
  • Taste fix: Frozen mango + banana in the smoothie — see recipes

Days 23–30: The "I Get It Now" Phase

By the final week, moringa had become part of my routine. Not in that forced, "I'm doing this because I should" way. But in the "this actually makes me feel better" way.

My overall energy throughout the month had levelled up noticeably. I was waking up easier, even before my alarm sometimes. My usual afternoon slump was barely a blip. My workouts felt stronger because I actually had energy for them instead of dragging myself through them.

The mental clarity thing surprised me most. I'm not talking about suddenly becoming Einstein or anything. But that brain fog I'd accepted as normal? Way less prevalent. I could focus on tasks longer without my mind wandering.

My cravings changed too. I was reaching for water more often, wanting healthier foods naturally. Nothing forced. Just subtle shifts in what sounded good.

The Things Nobody Mentions

Here's the real talk about moringa that you won't find in sponsored posts.

It doesn't work instantly

Anyone telling you they felt amazing after one dose is either exaggerating or experiencing placebo effect. Real benefits took at least a week for me — and meaningful changes came in week two.

The taste is challenging

You need to find your method. Smoothies worked for me. Some people do better with moringa tea. Some brave souls just mix it with water and slam it. Find what works — but don't give up after day one because of taste alone. See my recipes for options that genuinely taste good.

Quality matters hugely

I initially bought cheap moringa powder from a random online seller. It tasted worse and gave me an upset stomach. Switched to organic, tested moringa from a reputable brand. Complete difference. This is not the place to bargain hunt. See our moringa brands comparison for what to look for.

It can interact with medications

If you're on blood pressure medication, diabetes medication, or thyroid medication, talk to your doctor first. Moringa affects blood sugar and blood pressure. This isn't fear mongering — it's being responsible. For a full breakdown see our moringa side effects guide.

You might need to pee more

It's a natural diuretic. Not dramatically, but noticeably. Plan accordingly if you're going somewhere without bathroom access.

It's not a miracle cure

I still get tired if I don't sleep enough. I still need to eat well and exercise. Moringa supported my health — it didn't replace good habits. Think of it as a foundation layer, not a magic fix.

My Favourite Ways to Use Moringa (That Actually Taste Good)

After thirty days of experimentation, here are the recipes that worked. These are the methods that got me from "this tastes like a lawn" to actually looking forward to my morning dose.

Morning Energiser Smoothie

1 tsp moringa powder
1 frozen banana
½ cup frozen mango
1 handful spinach
1 cup almond milk
Squeeze of lemon juice
Tiny drizzle of honey
→ Blend until smooth. The citrus and mango completely cover the earthy taste.

Golden Moringa Latte

½ tsp moringa powder
½ tsp turmeric
¼ tsp cinnamon
Pinch of black pepper (helps absorption)
1 cup warm oat milk
Tiny bit of maple syrup
→ Whisk together or use a milk frother. Cozy and earthy in a good way.

Simple Moringa Tea

½ tsp moringa in hot (not boiling) water
Steep for 5 minutes
Fresh ginger + squeeze of lemon
→ Actually pleasant once you get used to it. The ginger gives it a nice kick.

Moringa Energy Balls

1 tbsp moringa powder
1 cup dates (pitted)
½ cup almonds
¼ cup coconut flakes
2 tbsp almond butter
→ Blitz in food processor, roll into balls, refrigerate. Tastes like dessert.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much moringa powder should I take daily?

Most studies suggest one to two teaspoons per day for adults. I started with one teaspoon and found that was enough. Start with half a teaspoon if you're sensitive to new supplements and work your way up over a week. More isn't always better, especially with something this nutrient dense.

When is the best time to take moringa powder?

Morning or early afternoon works best for most people. Taking it with breakfast helps with absorption and prevents any potential stomach upset. Avoid taking it late in the day if you're sensitive to its energising effects, as it might interfere with sleep for some people.

What does moringa powder taste like?

Honestly? It tastes earthy and slightly bitter, similar to matcha but grassier. The taste is strong when taken alone. Mixing it with fruits like mango, banana, or berries in smoothies masks the flavour well. Adding it to lattes with spices also helps.

Can I take moringa powder every day?

Yes, daily use is generally safe for most healthy adults. Many cultures have used moringa daily for thousands of years. However, if you're pregnant, nursing, or taking medications for blood pressure, diabetes, or thyroid conditions, talk to your doctor first.

How long does it take for moringa to work?

Based on my experience and research, most people notice subtle changes within five to seven days. More significant benefits typically appear after two weeks of consistent use. If you don't notice anything after three weeks, either the quality isn't good or it might not be the right fit for your body.

Does moringa powder have side effects?

Some people experience mild digestive upset, especially when starting or taking it on an empty stomach. It's a natural diuretic, so you might need to use the bathroom more frequently. If you take too much, you might experience nausea or discomfort. Start small and work your way up. For a full breakdown, see our moringa side effects guide.

Can moringa help with weight loss?

Moringa isn't a weight loss miracle, but it can support weight management. It's low in calories, nutrient dense, and may help regulate blood sugar, which can reduce cravings. I noticed more stable energy and fewer sugar cravings, which indirectly supported healthier eating habits.

Is moringa better than matcha or spirulina?

They're different, not better or worse. Matcha contains caffeine and has a different nutrient profile. Spirulina is higher in protein but lower in certain vitamins. Moringa has no caffeine, more vitamin A and calcium, and all nine essential amino acids. Check our full comparison guide for a detailed breakdown.

Where should I buy moringa powder?

Buy organic, third-party tested moringa from reputable brands. Check for certifications like USDA organic or non-GMO. Avoid suspiciously cheap options from unknown sellers. Quality matters significantly with moringa — I've tried cheap versions and quality ones, and the difference is noticeable. Our brands comparison guide covers the best options in Australia.

Can I take moringa while pregnant or breastfeeding?

This is something you absolutely need to discuss with your healthcare provider. While moringa has been traditionally used by nursing mothers in some cultures, there isn't enough research on its safety during pregnancy. Better to be safe and get professional medical advice.
