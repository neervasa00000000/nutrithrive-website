Storing moringa in Adelaide's climate

Adelaide experiences some of the hottest and driest summers in Australia, with temperatures regularly exceeding 38°C and occasional heatwaves above 45°C. Heat accelerates oxidation in moringa powder. You will notice this first as a colour change (vivid green → olive or yellow-green) and then as a flat, musty flavour.

  • Keep the sealed pouch in a cool, dark pantry or cupboard away from the stove.
  • After opening, press out excess air, reseal tightly, and consider refrigerating during SA summer.
  • Do not leave moringa in a hot car, even for an hour on a summer day.
  • Our UV-protective pouches help slow oxidation, but proper storage is the bigger factor.

What is the right dose of moringa powder?

Start with half a teaspoon (about 1.5g) daily for the first week to check tolerance, then increase to 1–2 teaspoons (3–6g) mixed into smoothies, yoghurt, or savoury dishes.
