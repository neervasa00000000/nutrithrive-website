## How this review judges “#1”

A page ranks #1 when it is the **most satisfying result** for the dominant query class — not merely “correct.” Competitors win when they offer:

1. Stronger **first-hand evidence** (bought / used / filmed / measured)
2. Stronger **entity trust** (reviews, brand, marketplace, UGC)
3. Better **format match** (video / shopping / community for brand queries)
4. Clearer **Needs Met** in the first screen (answer + proof, not essay)

**Honest global verdict:** After technical + text fixes, **none of the six Priority 1 pages would yet be published by SQT as “best on the internet.”** They are often *good desk-research pages*. #1 requires **primary evidence assets** most of them still lack.

Trustpilot, Facebook, YouTube (“I tried / honest review”), Amazon, Reddit outrank or surround text science pages for brand queries. Google often prefers **experience + UGC** over desk science for “does X work?”.

---

### 1. Why would Google still rank competitors higher?
- Format mismatch: brand/scam queries reward **video + user reviews**, not a long merchant blog.
- Competitors show **usage experience**; you disclose you **did not purchase packs**.
- Trustpilot/Amazon are **review entities**; Reddit/Facebook are **independent UGC**.
- Your commercial interest (selling powder) is disclosed — good for honesty, bad for perceived neutrality vs third-party reviewers.

### 2. What information do competitors have that we don't?
- Multi-week personal wear logs (weight, energy, skin reactions).
- Unboxing / application / wearability details.
- Aggregated star ratings and complaint themes (refunds, no effect, irritation).
- Side-by-side “before/after” narratives (even if weak science, they satisfy “experience intent”).

### 3. What evidence do competitors show that we don't?
- Product-in-hand photos/video of Glorenda/Healrize packs.
- Skin reaction photos.
- Receipt / order confirmation / wear calendar.
- You have: PubMed search log, ARTG desk checks, tables — **strong science synthesis, weak product handling proof**.

### 4. First-hand experience?
**Partial / weak for the product being reviewed.**  
Strong: oral powder handling, warehouse/batch awareness.  
Fatal line for #1: **“We did not purchase patch packs for this article.”** That is honest — and disqualifying vs “I tried 4 weeks” results.

### 5. Expertise?
**Medium–High** on delivery science, TGA framing, berberine interaction caution.
