We Upgraded Everything: 50+ SEO Fixes That Make NutriThrive Faster, Clearer, and Easier to Find

Updated: November 2025 • 8–10 min read • Evidence‑informed

Over the past few days we rebuilt our blog and product pages so they load faster, look better on social media, and are easier for Australians to find. Below is the full changelog—in plain English—plus links to key articles so you can see the improvements in action.

Highlights at a glance

  • Faster pages: Deferred analytics, preconnected fonts, and leaner markup on high‑traffic posts like Moringa Superfood Guide and Best Protein Powder 2025.
  • Correct canonicals: All pages now point to the final .html URL (no duplicates). Example: Black Tea.
  • Hreflang for Australia: Added en-AU + x-default across 90+ pages for better geo‑relevance.
  • Rich previews: Open Graph + Twitter Cards added or fixed on every blog post.
  • Broken links gone: Internal paths fixed (e.g., ../blog/ vs same‑directory), images consolidated and corrected.
  • Cleaner titles + meta: 28+ titles shortened to 50–70 chars; meta descriptions made unique and focused.

What we changed (with examples)

1) Removed meta‑refresh redirects and kept clean JS redirects

Meta refresh can confuse crawlers and users. We removed it everywhere and kept a simple JavaScript redirect where needed. Example: legacy entry page now points cleanly to the canonical article version.

2) Fixed duplicate pages with canonical URLs

Some URLs existed with and without .html. We now use a single canonical target and updated Open Graph/Twitter/Schema to match. See Moringa Superfood Guide and Best Moringa Powder.

3) Added OG + Twitter tags across the site

Every blog post now has consistent social preview tags. Try sharing Moringa vs Matcha or Black Tea Health Benefits to see crisp images and titles.

4) Rewrote titles and meta descriptions for CTR

We made titles more specific and user‑focused, trimmed length, and removed emojis/duplicates. Example updates in Dried Curry Leaves Ultimate Guide and Grow Moringa Tree.

5) Fixed broken internal links and image paths

We standardized shared/images, removed stray spaces (e.g., Curry1 .png → Curry1.png), and corrected relative links in Blog index and individual posts.

6) Performance upgrades on slow pages

We deferred GA, optimized font loading, and simplified DOM on heavy posts like Protein Guide and Moringa Brands Comparison.

Why this matters

  • Faster pages improve engagement and conversions.
  • Correct canonicals consolidate ranking signals to the right URL.
  • Hreflang helps Australians see our pages in AU searches.
  • Clean titles/meta improve click‑through rate from search and social.
  • Fixed links reduce bounce from 404s and 3xx chains.

FAQs

What is a canonical URL and why did you change it?

The canonical tells search engines which version of a page is the “main” version. We pointed every page to the final .html URL to avoid duplicates.

Why remove meta refresh?

Meta refresh can harm user experience and indexing. Clean redirects + correct canonicals are better for SEO.

What did you change for Australia?

We set lang="en-AU" and added hreflang en-AU and x-default so AU users get the correct version in Google.

Will pages load faster now?

Yes—deferred scripts and preconnects reduce blocking requests. We also removed duplicate CSS and heavy elements.
