    Snippet-Ready Content Examples

    FAQ (Featured Snippet)

    Q: What makes Darjeeling black tea special?

    Darjeeling tea is grown at high altitude in India's Himalayas. Its cool climate and unique terroir produce a sweet, grape-like "muscatel" aroma. Insects trigger the tea plants to release special compounds (like linalool), giving Darjeeling its signature muscatel flavor. It is often called the "Champagne of Teas" due to this refined taste.

    Q: Is Darjeeling tea healthy?

    Yes. Like other black teas, it's rich in antioxidants (theaflavins, catechins) that neutralize free radicals. Studies link daily black tea to lower heart disease and stroke risk. Darjeeling's polyphenols may support heart health and digestion, and its L-theanine can aid relaxation and focus.

    Q: How do I brew Darjeeling tea?

    Use fresh water heated to about 85–90 °C. Add ~1–2 teaspoons of loose leaf tea per cup. Steep for 3–5 minutes (shorter steep for lighter flavor, longer for strength). Strain and serve; enjoy it plain or with a splash of milk or honey.

    How to Brew Darjeeling Tea:

    1. Heat Water: Use filtered water heated to 85–90 °C (avoid full boiling).
    2. Measure Leaves: Add about 1–2 teaspoons of loose Darjeeling per 250 ml cup (or use 1 pyramid bag).
    3. Steep: Pour water and steep for 3–4 minutes (adjust for strength). A timer ensures consistency.
    4. Strain & Serve: Strain tea into a cup. Darjeeling shines plain, but a drop of milk, lemon or honey can complement its muscatel notes.
