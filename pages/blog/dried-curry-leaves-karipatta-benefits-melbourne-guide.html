What Are Curry Leaves (Karipatta)?

Curry leaves come from the curry tree (Murraya koenigii) and are a fragrant, citrus-scented herb prized in Indian and South Asian cooking. These glossy green leaves (called kari patta in Hindi) are often tempered in hot oil or ghee at the start of cooking to release their distinctive flavor.

üåø Quick Fact

Curry leaves are a staple in many South Indian recipes (for chutneys, dals, sambar, etc.) and are increasingly familiar to home cooks of all ages in Melbourne's Indian, Pakistani, Sri Lankan and Bangladeshi communities.

In each dish, curry leaves add a distinctive aroma and depth ‚Äì saut√©ing a few leaves in oil at the beginning can transform simple vegetables or lentils into an authentic curry. Fresh leaves are ideal, but dried curry leaves are widely used as a convenient substitute that preserves much of the fresh leaf's flavor and nutrients.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do dried curry leaves compare to fresh?

Dried curry leaves are more convenient and have a longer shelf life. Use about 2-3 times the amount of dried leaves compared to fresh for the same flavor intensity. They retain most of the nutritional benefits and essential oils.

What dishes can I use them in?

Perfect for curries, dals, sambars, rice dishes, and South Indian vegetable preparations. They add authentic flavor to any Indian dish and can be used in fusion cooking as well.

How should I store dried curry leaves?

Keep in an airtight container away from heat and light. Stored properly, they maintain their flavor for 6+ months. The fridge or freezer can extend freshness even longer.

Are curry leaves suitable for all diets?

Yes! Curry leaves are naturally vegan, gluten-free, and free of common allergens. They fit well into vegetarian, vegan, paleo, and most other dietary preferences.

Can children eat curry leaves?

Yes, in small amounts. They're very nutritious and safe as a spice/herb. Just chop finely or cook in dishes so they're easy to chew; some people strain the leaves out after cooking for toddlers.
