# Phase 6 — Executive summary

**Prepared for:** Nutri Thrive (non-technical reader)  
**Website:** https://nutrithrive.com.au  
**What we checked:** All **24 live URLs** listed in your `sitemap.xml`, using Firecrawl mapping and page-by-page scraping.

---

## 1) Overall site health score: **72 / 100**

**Why this score**

- **Strengths:** You have clear products, strong long-form guides, and pages that load successfully with sensible titles on most key screens.  
- **Deductions:** Several important pages tell Google the “real” page is your homepage when it is not (canonical tags), some product URLs don’t match their canonical links, and a few pages are missing normal search descriptions. These are fixable, but they matter.

Plain language: **the content is better than the plumbing right now**.

---

## 2) Top 3 strengths

1. **Excellent educational content** — Your big guides (brand comparisons, how-to-use, superfood comparisons) are unusually thorough. That helps people trust you before they buy.  
2. **Clear product pages** — Each main product has a simple headline, FAQs, and reasons to believe.  
3. **Local story** — Melbourne / Truganina positioning is understandable and repeated consistently in copy.

---

## 3) Top 3 critical problems (fix first)

1. **Wrong “canonical” signals on key pages** — Some important URLs effectively say “I’m the same as the homepage,” which can prevent those pages from ranking for their own topics (About, Melbourne local page, Newsletter, Shop index).  
2. **Product URL mismatches** — A shopper might be on one product link while the site tells Google a different product link is the “official” one. That can split reviews, rankings, and sharing.  
3. **Broken or weak sharing previews + risky internal links** — At least one guide has a broken social preview image pattern, and some pages link to articles that are not in your published sitemap (possible dead ends).

---

## 4) 30-day action plan (in order)

**Week 1 — Stop the bleeding (SEO plumbing)**

- Fix canonical tags on: About, Melbourne, Newsletter, Products index.  
- Fix product canonicals to match the real URLs (or add redirects so there is only one official URL).  
- Fix the usage guide social/share image metadata.

**Week 2 — Trust + completeness**

- Add proper short descriptions on Privacy, Shipping, Labs pages, and any blog missing a description.  
- Run a link check on Contact and footer areas; remove/repair links to missing pages.  
- Standardise your privacy URL pattern across the site.

**Week 3 — Conversion polish**

- Expand thin pages (newsletter + shipping) with simple, scannable sections.  
- Replace third-party image hosting on key shop surfaces where possible.  
- Clean FAQ heading structure for readability and accessibility.

**Week 4 — Measurement**

- Re-submit sitemap in Google Search Console after fixes.  
- Monitor: indexed pages, “duplicate canonical” issues, and click-through rates on top queries.

---

## 5) Tools to monitor ongoing health

- **Google Search Console** — indexing, queries, canonical coverage, rich results.  
- **Bing Webmaster Tools** — secondary index visibility (still used by many AU users via Edge).  
- **Ahrefs or Semrush** (paid) — backlink growth + keyword tracking (pick one).  
- **Screaming Frog** (desktop) — scheduled crawls to catch broken links and canonical drift.  
- **PageSpeed Insights / Web Vitals** — mobile performance checks on product and blog templates.

---

### Closing note

You are already doing the hard part—**useful content people actually want**. The next stage is mostly **technical housekeeping**: make sure Google sees the same URLs your customers see, and that every important page can explain itself in one clean sentence in search results.
