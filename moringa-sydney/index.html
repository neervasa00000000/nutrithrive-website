Storing moringa in Sydney's humid climate

Sydney's coastal humidity (especially January through March) can be problematic for open moringa pouches. Moisture causes clumping and accelerates oxidation far faster than heat alone. The visible sign is powder that clumps even when the bag is sealed, and a faint earthy smell replacing the fresh grassy aroma.

  • Keep sealed in the original pouch and store in a cool, dry pantry away from the kitchen sink or dishwasher.
  • After opening, press excess air out before resealing; consider a clip or rubber band.
  • Refrigeration during Sydney summer is a sensible precaution; bring to room temperature before opening to avoid condensation.
  • Do not leave the pouch in a car boot or warm garage in summer.
