About the miracle tree (Moringa oleifera)

Moringa oleifera is often called the miracle tree because its leaves are eaten as a everyday green in many countries. We dry those leaves into a fine powder — nothing else added. As a food, the leaf is rich in antioxidants and plant nutrients people use to round out smoothies and meals, not to replace medicine.

A common health benefit buyers mention is convenience: one or two teaspoons of moringa in the morning is an easy way to add greens without cooking. The benefits of moringa in the diet depend on your overall eating pattern; we do not make disease claims. If the flavour is new to you, expect a mild earthy taste — pairing with citrus, banana, or honey usually works well.

NutriThrive leaf powder is shade-dried (not sun-bleached), sieved, and packed in small batches in Melbourne. Vegan, naturally gluten-free, no preservatives.

Frequently asked questions

What is moringa oleifera powder?

It is dried, ground leaf from the moringa tree — one ingredient, no fillers. NutriThrive shade-dries leaves to help preserve green colour and aroma, then packs in Melbourne. Most customers stir one teaspoon into smoothies, juice, or yoghurt.

How much should I take?

Start with half a teaspoon daily for a week, then most adults use 1–2 teaspoons (about 3–10 g) if tolerated. Take with food. Our dosage guide has age notes and upper limits.

Is it safe?

Leaf powder is widely eaten as food. Each batch has an NMI heavy-metal and pesticide summary on file (link above). Pregnant or breastfeeding women and anyone on blood thinners or diabetes medication should ask their GP first.

Powder or capsules?

Powder shows colour and smell, costs less per gram of leaf, and lets you adjust the dose. Capsules suit travel but can hide stale powder. Compare powder vs capsules or Chemist Warehouse options.

Is moringa legal in Australia?

Yes — powder, leaves, and tea are legal to buy here. The TGA regulates health claims, not the plant. Why the “banned” rumour is wrong.
