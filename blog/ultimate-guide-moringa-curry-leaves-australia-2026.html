Moringa vs. Spirulina vs. Matcha

Part 3: Growing Moringa & Curry Leaves in Australia

Many of our customers want to know how to grow moringa leaves at home alongside their dried curry leaves.

Moringa Plant Growing Conditions

Moringa is a tropical native but can thrive in Australia if you follow these rules:

  • When is the best planting season? In Australia, aim for late spring (September to November) when the soil temperature consistently stays above 25°C.
  • How fast does moringa grow? In ideal conditions, it can reach 3 metres in its first year.
  • Protect from frost: This is the most critical factor. Moringa will die in a freeze. If you live in Victoria or Tasmania, you must protect from frost by using a greenhouse or moving pots indoors during winter.

The “combined routine” playbook: moringa + curry leaves in real Aussie meals

Readers love the idea of pairing moringa with curry leaves because it feels like a full-spectrum pantry upgrade: one is a nutrient-forward green powder, the other is an aroma-forward leafy herb. But the real win is behavioural. When your wellness tools show up in normal meals, you use them more often—and that’s where consistency turns into results.

Three beginner-friendly “starter recipes”

  • Green protein bowl (5 minutes): yoghurt or milk + oats, then stir moringa until smooth. Finish with lemon zest and crushed dried curry leaves for an aromatic twist.
  • Weeknight dal upgrade: temper onions and spices, add dried curry leaves early, then stir into lentils. At the end, add a small moringa scoop to thicken and lift the “fresh green” note.
  • Rice or quinoa “tadka finish”: toast curry leaves in oil, then drizzle over cooked grains. Pair with a side smoothie or iced drink that includes moringa for a balanced meal.

Cooking tip that saves you from disappointment: curry leaves reward hot oil, not cold-pan sprinkling. If your leaves smell faint, it’s usually because they didn’t get the short sizzle step. Moringa, on the other hand, rewards smooth mixing—whisk into a small warm liquid before adding to the rest.

How to prevent “taste fatigue”

Pick one breakfast routine and one savoury routine, then rotate them. For example: smoothies with moringa and honey on Monday/Wednesday, and curry-leaf dal on Tuesday/Thursday. The goal is not to eat the same thing every day—it is to keep the ritual familiar so you don’t abandon it when life gets busy.

Safety reminder for 2026: moringa is generally used as a food supplement, but it isn’t a medication. If you’re pregnant, managing thyroid health, or taking blood-thinning/diabetes medications, talk to a GP before changing your dose. Also store powders away from steam and humidity so the taste stays bright.
