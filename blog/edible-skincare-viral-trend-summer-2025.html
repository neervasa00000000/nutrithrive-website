Is Moringa good for acne-prone skin?

Yes, Moringa can be beneficial for acne-prone skin due to its anti-inflammatory properties and support for gut health. The gut-skin axis means that improving digestion and reducing inflammation internally can help reduce breakouts and redness from the inside out.

Can I use Moringa with retinol or vitamin C serums?

Yes, Moringa works well alongside topical skincare. While Moringa provides internal Vitamin A support, topical retinols and vitamin C serums work on the skin's surface. This creates a comprehensive approach - internal nourishment plus topical treatment.

How long until I see skin changes from Moringa?

Most people notice subtle improvements within 2-4 weeks of consistent use, such as brighter skin tone and less post-sun redness. More significant changes like improved texture and reduced fine lines typically appear after 6-8 weeks of daily consumption.

Is Moringa safe to take in Australian summer if I'm in the sun a lot?

Yes, Moringa is safe for summer use and can actually help protect against sun damage. Its high antioxidant content helps combat UV-induced oxidative stress. However, always use sunscreen and sun protection alongside internal skincare for comprehensive protection.
