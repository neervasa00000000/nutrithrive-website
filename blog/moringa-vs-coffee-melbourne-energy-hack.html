3) The Science: How Caffeine Hijacks Your Energy System

Here's the simplest truth:

Caffeine doesn't give energy. It blocks tiredness.

Caffeine is an adenosine antagonist

Adenosine is a natural signal in your brain that builds throughout the day and makes you sleepy.

Caffeine binds to adenosine receptors without activating them, blocking the tired signal.

So you feel "awake" ‚Äî but your body hasn't solved fatigue.

The caffeine crash isn't random ‚Äî it's adenosine debt

While caffeine blocks receptors, adenosine still accumulates.

When caffeine wears off (often 4‚Äì6 hours later), adenosine floods the receptors and causes a brutal crash.

Coffee also activates the stress response

Your brain interprets artificial stimulation as:

"Emergency. Stay alert."

So it triggers the HPA axis and releases:
