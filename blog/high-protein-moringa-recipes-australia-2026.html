Recipe 1: Moringa Protein Breakfast — Avo Smash with Hemp Seeds (22g Protein)

This is the upgrade on Melbourne's weekend staple. Hemp seeds add a clean 10 g of protein per 3-tablespoon serve, and moringa's iron and beta-carotene make this genuinely more than a café indulgence.

Moringa Avo Smash with Hemp Seeds

⏱ 5 min 🍽 1 serve 💪 ~22 g protein (with eggs)

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices sourdough or wholegrain toast
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1 tsp NutriThrive moringa powder
  • 3 tbsp hemp seeds
  • ½ lemon, juiced
  • Salt, chilli flakes, black pepper
  • 2 poached or soft-boiled eggs (optional, adds ~12 g protein)

Method:

  1. Mash avocado with moringa, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt until combined. Don't over-mash — some texture holds up better.
  2. Spread on toast. Scatter hemp seeds and chilli flakes.
  3. Top with eggs if using. Season and serve immediately.
Pro tip: Mash the moringa directly into the avocado before adding lemon. This distributes the colour evenly and prevents it sitting on top as a green streak.

Recipe 2: Moringa Protein Latte — Post-Workout Green Silk (8–18g Protein)

Melbourne's gym crowd has been replacing post-training coffee with this for good reason. No caffeine crash, no acidity, and the magnesium in macadamia milk helps muscles relax after a hard session.

Green Silk Moringa Latte

⏱ 4 min 🍽 1 serve 💪 ~8 g protein (add collagen for more)

Ingredients:

  • 250 ml macadamia milk (or oat milk for higher protein)
  • 1 tsp NutriThrive moringa powder
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp local honey or maple syrup
  • 1 scoop collagen peptides (optional — adds 8–10 g protein)

Method:

  1. Heat milk to about 65 °C (just before steaming — do not boil).
  2. Whisk moringa and cinnamon into a small amount of cold milk first to form a paste. This prevents clumping.
  3. Combine with remaining warm milk. Whisk well or froth with a handheld frother.
  4. Sweeten to taste. Drink within 5 minutes for best colour.
Pro tip: The paste-first method is the only reliable way to get lump-free moringa in hot drinks. Skip it and you'll have green specks floating on the surface.

Recipe 3: Moringa Protein Smoothie Australia — Tropical Mango (28g Protein)

QLD summers and Brisbane mornings call for something cold. Frozen mango and pineapple bring digestive enzymes that pair well with moringa's plant iron. Add a protein source and you have a complete meal.

Tropical Mango Moringa Smoothie

⏱ 3 min 🍽 1 serve 💪 ~28 g protein (with Greek yoghurt + protein powder)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup frozen mango chunks
  • ¼ cup frozen pineapple
  • 1 tsp NutriThrive moringa powder
  • 150 g Greek yoghurt (adds ~15 g protein)
  • 1 scoop vanilla protein powder (adds ~20–25 g protein)
  • 200 ml coconut water
  • Small handful fresh mint (optional)

Method:

  1. Add all ingredients to blender. Blend on high for 45 seconds until completely smooth.
  2. Taste — add more moringa for deeper green, or more mango if too earthy.
  3. Serve immediately over ice.
Pro tip: The mango and pineapple are strong enough to fully mask moringa's earthiness — this is the recipe to start with if you're new to moringa and nervous about the taste.

Recipe 4: Moringa High-Protein Scrambled Eggs — Fastest Breakfast (22g Protein)

Three eggs give you about 18 g of complete protein. Moringa adds iron and B vitamins. This is the fastest high-protein weekday breakfast that doesn't involve protein powder.

Moringa Scrambled Eggs

⏱ 6 min 🍽 1 serve 💪 ~18–22 g protein

Ingredients:

  • 3 free-range eggs
  • ¾ tsp NutriThrive moringa powder
  • 2 tbsp milk or cream
  • 1 tsp butter or extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Chilli flakes, microgreens, or feta to serve

Method:

  1. Whisk eggs, milk, moringa, salt and pepper until the moringa is fully incorporated. The mix will be a pale green — that's correct.
  2. Heat butter in a non-stick pan over medium-low. Pour in egg mix.
  3. Stir slowly and continuously with a spatula, removing from heat occasionally. Pull off heat while still slightly underdone — residual heat finishes them.
  4. Serve on toast with your choice of toppings.
Pro tip: Whisk moringa into the eggs before cooking, not sprinkled on top. The egg protein wraps around the moringa particles and the colour blends seamlessly — no visible green streaks.

Recipe 5: Moringa Greek Yoghurt Bowl

Greek yoghurt is the most underrated protein food in Australian fridges. 200 g delivers roughly 18 g of protein before you add anything. Moringa turns it into a genuine nutritional powerhouse.

Moringa Greek Yoghurt Bowl

⏱ 3 min 🍽 1 serve 💪 ~22–28 g protein

Ingredients:

  • 200 g full-fat Greek yoghurt
  • 1 tsp NutriThrive moringa powder
  • 1 tbsp nut butter (almond or peanut)
  • 1 tsp honey
  • Granola, sliced banana, or berries to top
  • 2 tbsp hemp seeds (optional — adds 6 g protein)

Method:

  1. Stir moringa into yoghurt thoroughly. It takes about 30 seconds of vigorous stirring to fully combine — do not rush this step.
  2. Transfer to a bowl. Add nut butter, honey, and toppings.
  3. Eat immediately or refrigerate for up to 2 hours (colour intensifies over time).
Pro tip: Moringa and yoghurt make a surprisingly good savoury dip — skip the honey, add garlic, lemon, and olive oil. Serve with flatbread or veggie sticks.

Recipe 6: Moringa High-Protein Dinner — Green Dhal (22g Protein Per Serve)

Dhal is arguably the world's best high-protein, high-iron weeknight dinner. Add moringa off the heat — stirring it into the finished dhal preserves far more of its vitamin C than cooking it in.

Moringa Green Dhal

⏱ 30 min 🍽 4 serves 💪 ~22 g protein per serve

Ingredients:

  • 250 g red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 can (400 ml) coconut milk
  • 400 ml vegetable stock
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tsp ground cumin + 1 tsp turmeric + 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp olive oil or ghee
  • 1½ tsp NutriThrive moringa powder (added off heat)
  • Salt, lime juice, fresh coriander to serve
  • Optional: 1 tsp NutriThrive dried curry leaves in the tempering oil — see our dried curry leaves product page for pack size and use tips

Method:

  1. Heat oil in a heavy pot. Fry onion until golden (7–8 min). Add garlic, ginger, and spices; cook 2 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Add lentils, stock, and coconut milk. Bring to a boil, then reduce and simmer 20 min, stirring occasionally, until lentils are completely soft.
  3. Remove from heat. Stir in moringa powder and lime juice. Let sit 2 minutes.
  4. Serve with rice or flatbread, topped with fresh coriander.
Pro tip: Always add moringa after removing from heat. Simmering it destroys most of its vitamin C within a few minutes — but the minerals and plant protein survive. The colour stays vivid green rather than turning army-drab.

Recipe 7: Moringa Pesto Pasta

Classic pesto gets an iron and calcium upgrade. Use chickpea or lentil pasta and you're looking at 30+ g of protein in a single bowl without any meat.

Moringa Pesto Pasta

⏱ 15 min 🍽 2 serves 💪 ~30 g protein per serve (with chickpea pasta)

Ingredients (pesto):

  • 1 large bunch fresh basil (about 50 g leaves)
  • 1 tsp NutriThrive moringa powder
  • 50 g parmesan, grated (adds ~18 g protein)
  • 30 g pine nuts or cashews
  • 1 small garlic clove
  • 80–100 ml extra virgin olive oil
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • Salt to taste

Ingredients (bowl):

  • 200 g chickpea or lentil pasta (dry weight)
  • Cherry tomatoes, baby spinach, or grilled zucchini to serve

Method:

  1. Cook pasta per packet directions. Reserve ¼ cup pasta water before draining.
  2. Blend all pesto ingredients until smooth. Taste — add salt or lemon to balance.
  3. Toss warm pasta with pesto, adding pasta water by the tablespoon to loosen.
  4. Serve with vegetables and extra parmesan.
Pro tip: Start with ¾ tsp moringa in the pesto and taste before adding the full teaspoon — basil has varying strength. You want moringa as a background note that deepens the green colour, not a dominant flavour.

Recipe 8: Highest Protein Moringa Recipe — Almond-Crusted Salmon (40g Protein)

Salmon is one of the highest-protein, omega-3-dense foods available in Australian supermarkets. The moringa-almond crust adds minerals and a visual green edge that looks far more impressive than it is to make.

Moringa Almond-Crusted Salmon

⏱ 20 min 🍽 2 serves 💪 ~40 g protein per serve

Ingredients:

  • 2 salmon fillets (about 180 g each)
  • 50 g raw almonds, roughly crushed
  • 1 tsp NutriThrive moringa powder
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley or coriander, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp lemon zest
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Mix crushed almonds, moringa, parsley, lemon zest, and olive oil in a bowl until combined.
  3. Season salmon fillets. Press the moringa-almond mix firmly onto the top surface of each fillet.
  4. Bake 12–14 minutes until crust is golden and salmon just flakes. Do not overcook — the crust will look done before the fish does.
  5. Serve with roasted vegetables or a green salad.
Pro tip: The almond crust benefits from pressing it on firmly before baking — if it's loose, it slides off. A brief 2-minute rest after removing from the oven helps it set.

Recipe 9: Moringa Protein Energy Balls

The meal-prep snack that solves the 3 pm problem. These keep in the fridge for a week and freeze for three months. The dates bind everything without refined sugar, and the protein powder + nut butter combination makes them genuinely filling.

Moringa Protein Energy Balls

⏱ 15 min + 30 min chill 🍽 Makes 14–16 balls 💪 ~7 g protein per 2 balls

Ingredients:

  • 150 g Medjool dates, pitted
  • 80 g rolled oats
  • 2 tbsp almond butter or peanut butter
  • 1 scoop vanilla protein powder (~25 g)
  • 1½ tsp NutriThrive moringa powder
  • 2 tbsp desiccated coconut (plus extra for rolling)
  • 1–2 tbsp water (if mix is too dry)

Method:

  1. Blend dates alone in a food processor until a paste forms.
  2. Add oats, nut butter, protein powder, moringa, and coconut. Pulse until combined — you want a dough that holds together when pressed. Add water a tablespoon at a time if too dry.
  3. Roll into golf-ball-sized portions. Roll in extra desiccated coconut.
  4. Refrigerate on a tray for at least 30 minutes to firm up.
Pro tip: Don't over-blend after adding oats — you want some texture from the oats, not a completely smooth paste. The chewiness is part of what makes these satisfying.

Recipe 10: Moringa Chickpea Stir-Fry

Chickpeas are Australia's most underused protein source — 15 g of protein per cup, cheap, and available canned in every supermarket. This stir-fry takes under 15 minutes and works as a side dish or a light main.

Moringa Chickpea Stir-Fry

⏱ 12 min 🍽 2 serves as a main, 4 as a side 💪 ~20 g protein per main serve

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (400 g) chickpeas, drained and patted dry
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin + 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 100 g baby spinach or kale
  • 1 tsp NutriThrive moringa powder (added at end)
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • Salt and chilli flakes to taste
  • Serve with: flatbread, rice, or poached eggs

Method:

  1. Heat oil in a large pan over medium-high. Add chickpeas in a single layer. Cook undisturbed for 3–4 minutes until one side is golden and slightly crispy.
  2. Add garlic, cumin, and paprika. Toss and cook 2 more minutes.
  3. Add spinach or kale. Stir until just wilted (1–2 minutes).
  4. Remove from heat. Stir in moringa powder and lemon juice. Taste and season.
  5. Serve immediately.
Pro tip: Crispy chickpeas are the goal — dry them properly before adding to the pan, and don't stir for the first 3–4 minutes. Wet chickpeas steam instead of crisping and taste completely different.

These recipes are calibrated for shade-dried, fine-ground moringa leaf powder.
