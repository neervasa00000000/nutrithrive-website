Safety, Dosage, and Contraindications

While Moringa is a food, its potency requires respect. Here's what you need to know before starting.

Start Low, Go Slow

Moringa is high in fiber and has a mild laxative effect. Beginners should start with 1/2 teaspoon (or 1 capsule) daily for the first week to avoid "digestive surprise."

Thyroid Medication

Moringa can interfere with the absorption of Levothyroxine. Take your medication in the morning and Moringa at lunch (at least 4 hours apart).

Pregnancy

While the leaf is generally safe, avoid Moringa root or bark as they contain compounds that can stimulate uterine contractions. Always consult your GP before use during pregnancy.

Blood Thinners

Moringa contains Vitamin K, which promotes clotting. If you are on Warfarin, monitor your intake closely and consult your healthcare provider.

At-Home "DIY" Tests for Your Moringa Powder

These simple checks help you quickly judge whether your moringa powder is worth keeping.

1. Colour & Smell Test (10 seconds)

Put ½ tsp on a white plate. Check the colour in natural light: bright green and grassy smell = good. Dull, brown, musty smell = likely oxidised or poor quality.

2. Water Glass Test

Add ½ tsp to a clear glass of water and stir. High-quality moringa (like NutriThrive) will suspend fairly evenly with fine particles. Poor-quality powder often forms sticky clumps or shows bits of stem that never break down.

3. Taste Micro-Test

Dip a wet fingertip into the powder and taste a tiny amount. It should be earthy, slightly bitter but clean (like strong green tea or spinach). If you get burnt, fishy, or chemical notes, don't keep using it. These tests don't replace lab results, but they're useful first filters when comparing moringa brands at home.
