Top Moringa Companies in Australia (2026)

Published March 31, 2026 • Australian edition

Home › Blog › Top moringa companies — Australia 2026

Moringa oleifera, often called the “miracle tree,” is a nutrient-dense plant gaining steady popularity in Australia. Leaves and seeds are rich in protein, vitamins A, C and B-complex, and minerals such as calcium, iron and potassium. As a rough illustration, dried moringa leaf can be very iron-dense compared with common greens (exact numbers depend on variety and drying method). Globally, bulk moringa powder often trades in roughly the USD $15–$40 per kg range for commodity grades; Australian retail prices move up or down with organic certification, pack size, promotions and whether the product is grown locally or imported.

Below we profile leading names that supply Australian customers, then give apples-to-apples price comparisons where public list prices allow. Prices and pack sizes change often—always confirm on the seller’s site before you buy.

Australian moringa producers and suppliers (profiles)

Moringa Farm Australia (Cairns, QLD)

Family-run since 2011, growing and processing 100% Australian moringa. Products include capsules, powders and oils on-site. Example public pricing: a 140 g (roughly 280-capsule) pack of Australian-grown capsules around $92. The farm stresses freshness—leaves harvested weekly and cold-processed. Strength: true local provenance and traceability. Limitation: season and farm scale cap volume; prices reflect small-batch production.

Medikonda Nutrients (QLD)

Describes itself as a major Australian organic moringa manufacturer and exporter, with a large plantation and GMP-certified processing. Focus on bulk powder, oil and animal-grade lines. Retail list prices are often quote-based; their materials reference wholesale powder near $15–$40 / kg depending on grade and volume. Strength: vertical integration and export scale. Limitation: primarily B2B; household buyers may need quotes and minimums.

Moringa Products Australia (Melbourne, VIC)

Active since 2016 as a retailer and private-label style brand. Sources leaf from Queensland and southern India; range spans powders, capsules, oils and blends. Example: organic leaf capsules near $55–$93 for 60 depending on SKU. They publish large review counts (hundreds of entries) with very high average ratings. Strength: range and social proof. Limitation: not exclusively Australian-grown leaf; mid-to-premium pricing.

Organic Moringa Australia (Sydney, NSW)

Online brand emphasising USDA / Indian / EU organic credentials and Australian ownership. Positions around certified organic oil, powder and capsules. Public prices may be quote-only; testimonials on their site skew five-star. They also educate buyers that moringa is a novel food under FSANZ rules. Strength: certification transparency. Limitation: regulatory framing for “supplement” language; narrower SKU line than big retailers.

Bulk Superfoods — Health Co (Perth, WA)

House brand on healthco.com.au with imported superfoods. Their 500 g organic leaf powder (southern India) is often listed around $59.95 with frequent promos near $24.95. Strength: aggressive promotional $/100 g. Limitation: import origin; multi-brand retailer with lighter product storytelling than a single-ingredient specialist.

Indus Farms (India; AU via Exordium)

Bulk-oriented Indian producer whose lines are distributed in Australia (Exordium Services). Example: 60 vegan capsules near $24.99. Certifications such as ISO/HACCP and halal are highlighted. Strength: low capsule cost. Limitation: long supply chain from India; brand less visible on shelf than domestic labels.

Legend Age (Sydney) — Nutra Nourished

Wellness retailer stocking Nutra Nourished “organic pure moringa leaf capsules” labelled made in Australia. Example bundle: 2×60 capsules around $84.95 on promotion (vs higher “was” pricing). Large marketing claims on customer counts. Strength: Aussie-made capsule story and bundling. Limitation: higher $/dose vs import discounters; fewer independent third-party review aggregates.

Orgo All-Natural (Ghaziabad, India)

Ingredient exporter marketing to Australian buyers as a wholesale moringa powder partner. Strength: volume and organic farming narrative. Limitation: not Australia-based; end consumers usually buy through brokers or white-label brands.

Other notable names

Bondi Beach Tea Co lists Australian-grown moringa powder around $59 / 500 g. Global names like Kuli Kuli or Comvita have niche Australian listings but are not core to the domestic moringa aisle.

Advantages and weaknesses (summary)

  • Local growers (e.g. Moringa Farm Australia, parts of Medikonda’s story): traceability and “grown in Australia” appeal; higher $/g.
  • Importer retailers (Bulk Superfoods, some SKUs from Moringa Products AU): sharp promotional $/100 g; check origin and testing.
  • Manufacturer-direct (Nutri Thrive): India-sourced leaf, specifications controlled by the brand, $10.50 / 100 g entry point, Melbourne dispatch—with room to scale packs (200 g, bundles) on the same product page.
  • Bulk brokers (Medikonda, Orgo): best for businesses; household buyers compare MOQ and landed cost.

No single supplier wins every use case. Match origin, organic/lab claims, format (powder vs capsule) and $/100 g to your budget and values.
