Step 1 of 5

Identify Your Primary Goal

This is the most important step. A greens powder optimised for gut health is completely different from one optimised for protein or energy. Buying without knowing your goal is how people end up dissatisfied.

For Energy (Without Caffeine)

Look for: High iron, B vitamins (B6, B12), magnesium, adaptogens

Best options: Moringa ($0.22/serve), complete blends with adaptogens

Avoid: Barley grass only — low in the nutrients that drive energy

For Gut Health & Digestion

Look for: Probiotics (3+ strains), digestive enzymes, prebiotics (inulin), L-glutamine

Best options: EHP Labs OxyGreens, AG1, GRNS

Avoid: Single-ingredient powders without probiotics

For Protein (Vegans & Athletes)

Look for: High protein content (50%+ of weight), complete amino acid profile

Best options: Spirulina (60% protein), moringa (27% protein)

Avoid: Wheatgrass, barley grass — low protein

For Focus & Cognitive Energy

Look for: L-theanine, caffeine, antioxidants (EGCG)

Best options: Ceremonial matcha ($1.20/serve)

Avoid: Caffeine-free options if you want active mental focus

For Budget & Daily Nutrition

Look for: Price per serve under $0.50, broad micronutrient profile

Best options: Moringa ($0.22/serve), barley grass ($0.30/serve)

Avoid: Complete blends at $3–5/serve — excessive for basic daily nutrition

For Heavy Metal Detox

Look for: Chlorella or spirulina (both bind heavy metals)

Best options: Pure chlorella, pure spirulina

Avoid: Complete blends (too dilute for detox purposes)

Step 2 of 5

Read the Ingredient List (Green Flags vs Red Flags)

The ingredient list tells you more than the marketing on the front of the pack. Here's exactly what to look for.

Green Flags (Buy With Confidence)

Specific ingredient amounts listed (e.g., "3g spirulina" not just "spirulina")

Short, recognisable ingredient list (5–15 items)

Natural sweeteners only: stevia, monk fruit

Third-party lab tested (certificate of analysis available)

Organic certification or verified sourcing

Probiotic strains named specifically (e.g., "Lactobacillus acidophilus")

Clear "best before" or batch date on packaging

Red Flags (Think Twice)

"Proprietary blend" with no individual amounts — brand is hiding underdosing

Artificial sweeteners (sucralose, acesulfame K) — unnecessary and potentially harmful

Fillers: maltodextrin, cellulose, silicon dioxide as main ingredients

No testing information or certificates of analysis

Vague descriptions: "superfood blend," "greens complex" with no specifics

Protein listed fourth or lower in the ingredients — the dose is likely negligible

No money-back guarantee

What "Proprietary Blend" Really Means

When a brand lists "Super Greens Blend 5,000mg" without individual amounts, they can legally include 4,900mg of cheap wheatgrass and 100mg of the premium ingredients they advertise. You have no way to know. This is standard practice for budget brands trying to appear premium.

Step 3 of 5

Calculate Price Per Serve (Don't Get Fooled)

Sticker price is meaningless without knowing the serving size and number of serves. A "$20 bag" is not a deal if it only has 10 serves.

Formula: Total Price ÷ Number of Serves = Price Per Serve

Example B: $11 ÷ 50 serves = $0.22/serve (NutriThrive Moringa)

Step 4 of 5

Read Reviews Properly

Online reviews are valuable, but you need to filter for useful signal and ignore noise.

Look For (Signal)

Reviews from people who've used it for 30+ days (not first impressions)

Specific outcomes: "energy improved after 2 weeks," "bloating gone in a week"

Mentions of taste and mixability in detail

Comparisons to other products they've tried

Negative reviews that still describe the product accurately (shows real feedback)

Ignore (Noise)

5-star reviews shorter than 2 sentences — likely not real

Reviews that use the brand's exact marketing language ("amazing superfood blend!")

Extreme outliers in either direction without explanation

Reviews that mention receiving a discount or free product (biased)

Where to Find Reliable Reviews

  • ProductReview.com.au — moderated, Australia-specific, hard to fake
  • Reddit (r/FitnessAustralia, r/supplements) — candid community feedback
  • Google Reviews — useful for overall brand trust
  • Brand website — least reliable; treat as marketing material
Step 5 of 5

Check Availability & Returns Policy

Before you commit, make sure the logistics work for you.

Availability Questions to Ask

  • Is it available in stores if you want it immediately, or online only?
  • What's the shipping time from order to delivery? (NutriThrive ships same day from Melbourne — 1–3 days Australia-wide)
  • Is there a subscription option with a discount?
  • What's the re-order process?

Returns Policy — Non-Negotiable

Any reputable greens powder brand should offer at least a 7-day money-back guarantee. AG1 offers 60 days. NutriThrive offers 7 days. Avoid brands with no return policy — especially for a product you haven't tried before.

Subscription vs One-Off Purchase

Try one-off first. Subscriptions save money but you're locked in before you know if the product works for you. Buy one bag or one month's supply. If you love it, then subscribe. Most brands make cancellation harder than sign-up.

The Golden Rule: Start Simple

The biggest mistake people make is buying three different greens powders at once because they can't decide. Instead:

  1. Pick one product that matches your primary goal
  2. Use it consistently for 30 days
  3. Note what improved (energy, digestion, skin, immunity)
  4. Then decide whether to stick with it or try something else

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I look for when buying greens powder in Australia?
Prioritise: clear ingredient amounts (no proprietary blends), third-party lab testing, a transparent price per serve, and a returns policy. Secondary considerations are taste, availability, and whether you need probiotics included.
How do I calculate price per serve?
Divide total price by number of serves. A $11/100g bag with 50 serves (2g each) = $0.22/serve. A $135 bag with 30 serves = $4.50/serve. Never compare total bag price — always compare per serve.
Should I choose a complete blend or a single-ingredient powder?
Complete blends (AG1, GRNS) are more convenient and comprehensive, but more expensive. Single ingredients (moringa, spirulina) give you full transparency and better value. Start with a single ingredient if you're budget-conscious or want simplicity.
Which greens powder is best for gut health in Australia?
OxyGreens, AG1, and GRNS — all contain probiotic strains and digestive enzymes. Single-ingredient options like moringa or spirulina do not have probiotics, so they won't address gut health directly.
How long should I try a greens powder before deciding if it works?
Give it at least 30 days of daily use. Digestion improvements appear within 1–2 weeks. Energy improvements typically show at 2–3 weeks. Immunity and long-term benefits take 2–3 months. One week is not enough to judge most greens powders.
Are all-natural greens powders better than blends with flavouring?
Not necessarily. Natural flavourings (fruit extracts, vanilla) are fine and improve palatability. What to avoid are artificial sweeteners (sucralose, acesulfame-K) and artificial colours. A product with natural flavouring that you actually drink daily beats a "pure" product that tastes terrible and sits in your cupboard.
