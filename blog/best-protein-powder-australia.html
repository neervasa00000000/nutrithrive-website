📊 Protein Powder Myths: Busted by Science

MYTH #1: "You need protein powder to build muscle"

TRUTH: Protein powder is convenience, not necessity. 200g chicken + 3 eggs + Greek yogurt = 140g protein — no powder required.

MYTH #2: "More protein = more muscle"

TRUTH: Beyond 2.2g/kg bodyweight, extra protein becomes expensive urine. Sweet spot: 1.8–2.2g/kg for active Australians.

MYTH #3: "Whey is better than plant protein"

TRUTH: Both build muscle equally when total protein is matched. A 12-week study showed identical muscle gains between pea protein and whey.

MYTH #4: "Drink protein within 30 minutes post-workout"

TRUTH: The "anabolic window" is 24–48 hours, not 30 minutes. Total daily protein intake matters far more than timing.

MYTH #5: "Protein powder damages your kidneys"

TRUTH: No evidence in healthy adults. High-protein diets (2.5g/kg) are safe for healthy kidneys. Caution: pre-existing kidney disease — consult your doctor.

❓ FAQ: Protein Powder Australia

How much protein powder should I take daily?

1–2 scoops (25–50g protein) depending on your total daily needs. Calculate: bodyweight (kg) × 1.6–2.2 = daily protein target. An 80kg person needs 128–176g/day; if meals provide 100g, powder adds 28–76g.

Can protein powder help with weight loss?

Yes, indirectly. Protein increases satiety and preserves muscle during a calorie deficit. Best choices for weight loss: high protein (25g+), low calories (<120), low sugar (<2g). Recommendation: Evolve WPI + moringa for metabolism support.

What's the difference between WPI and WPC?

WPI (Whey Isolate): 90%+ protein, minimal lactose, fast absorption, more expensive.
WPC (Whey Concentrate): 70–80% protein, more lactose, slower absorption, cheaper.
Choose WPI if lactose intolerant or in a cutting phase. Choose WPC if budget-conscious and not lactose sensitive.

Is protein powder safe for women?

Absolutely. Protein powder doesn't cause bulk or hormonal changes. Women benefit from muscle tone, stronger bones, better recovery, and weight management. Recommended: Maxine's Burn, Bare Blends Vegan, or any WPI.

Can I take protein powder if I'm lactose intolerant?

Yes — choose WPI (minimal lactose, usually tolerated), vegan protein (zero lactose), or add a lactase enzyme. Best options: True Protein WPI or Bare Blends Vegan. Adding moringa also supports digestion.

Why does my protein powder taste bad?

Common reasons: wrong liquid ratio (use 300ml per scoop), cheap artificial flavours, expired powder, or wrong liquid base. Fix: try ON Gold Standard, blend with frozen berries + 1 tsp moringa.

Can I cook/bake with protein powder?

Yes! Use in pancakes (replace ¼ flour), oatmeal (stir in 1 scoop), protein balls (mix with dates, nuts, moringa), and smoothies. Heat can denature some proteins but they remain nutritionally valuable.

What's the shelf life of protein powder?

Unopened: 18–24 months (check "best before"). Opened: 3–6 months in cool, dry place. Signs of spoilage: clumping, off smell, discoloration. Store in an airtight container away from moisture and heat.

Do I need protein powder if I eat meat?

Not necessarily. If you consistently hit 1.6–2.2g protein/kg from food, powder is optional. It's most useful for convenience, post-workout timing, hitting high athlete targets, or vegetarian/vegan diets.
