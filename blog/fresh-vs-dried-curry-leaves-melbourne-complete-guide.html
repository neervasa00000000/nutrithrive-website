Cooking Conversions & Best Practices

If you're switching from fresh to dried curry leaves in your recipes, follow these simple rules:

Conversion Guidelines

  • Double or triple the quantity: Dried leaves are less aromatic, so use more
  • Fry early in hot oil/ghee: Add dried curry leaves right at the beginning of tempering
  • Crumble for better infusion: Crumbling dried leaves before adding releases more aroma
  • Avoid soaking: Dried curry leaves don't need to be rehydrated ‚Äî oil does the work
  • Store smart: Keep in an airtight jar in a cool, dark cupboard

Best Cooking Methods

For Tempering (Tadka)

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan
  2. Add dried curry leaves (2-3x more than fresh)
  3. Fry until crispy and aromatic (30-60 seconds)
  4. Add to dal, curry, or rice dishes

For Curry Leaf Tea

  1. Use 15-20 dried leaves per cup
  2. Boil in water for 5-7 minutes
  3. Strain and enjoy with honey

Storage Tips for Maximum Freshness

Fresh Curry Leaves Storage

  • Wrap in damp paper towel
  • Store in refrigerator crisper drawer
  • Use within 3-5 days for best flavor
  • Freeze for longer storage (up to 3 months)

Dried Curry Leaves Storage

  • Keep in airtight container
  • Store in cool, dark pantry
  • Maintains potency for 6-12 months
  • Check for moisture and replace if needed

What's the difference between karipatta and curry leaves?

Karipatta is the Hindi name for curry leaves (Murraya koenigii). They're the same plant - karipatta is just the traditional Indian name.
