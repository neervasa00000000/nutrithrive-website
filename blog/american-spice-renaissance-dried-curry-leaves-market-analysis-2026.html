1) What Curry Leaves Actually Are (And What They're Not)

Curry leaves are not curry powder.

Curry powder is a spice blend. Curry leaves are a real herb with a naturally complex aroma‚Äîoften described as:

  • citrusy and bright
  • slightly nutty when toasted
  • savory and deeply aromatic

In traditional cooking, curry leaves are not used as garnish. They're used at the beginning of cooking in hot oil or ghee through a method known as tadka/tempering. This step releases their aromatic oils and spreads flavor throughout the entire dish.

That's why curry leaves aren't optional in many recipes‚Äîthey're the backbone.

Frequently Asked Questions: Curry Leaves

What are curry leaves used for?

Curry leaves are used to add deep aroma and authentic flavor to Indian and South Asian dishes. They're typically toasted in hot oil or ghee at the start of cooking to release their fragrance.

Are curry leaves the same as curry powder?

No. Curry leaves are a real herb from the curry leaf tree (Murraya koenigii). Curry powder is a spice blend made from ingredients like turmeric, cumin, coriander, and fenugreek.

Why do dried curry leaves have less aroma?

Many dried curry leaves are sun-dried and stored for long periods, causing their aromatic oils to fade. Shade-drying and airtight storage help retain aroma.

How do you use dried curry leaves properly?

Toast them in hot oil or ghee first (tadka/tempering). Crushing them slightly also improves aroma release. Add them early so they infuse the dish.

Where can I buy curry leaves in the USA?

Fresh curry leaves are mostly found in Indian grocery stores in major cities. Dried curry leaves are widely available online, but quality varies significantly.

Do curry leaves expire?

They don't "expire" quickly like fresh leaves, but aroma fades over time. For best results, store dried curry leaves airtight away from heat and light.
