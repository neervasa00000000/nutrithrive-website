Dried curry leaves (karipatta) are incredibly versatile. Here are 10 quick and easy ways to use them in your everyday cooking.

10 Ways to Use Dried Curry Leaves

  1. 1. Tempering (Tadka) for Dal & Curries

    Heat oil/ghee, add mustard seeds and cumin, then crumble 10-15 dried curry leaves. Fry 20-30 seconds until fragrant. Pour over cooked dal or add at the start of curries.

  2. 2. Lemon Rice & Coconut Rice

    Fry dried curry leaves with mustard seeds, then mix into cooked rice with lemon juice or coconut. Perfect for quick South Indian-style rice dishes.

  3. 3. Curry Leaf Powder (Karuveppilai Podi)

    Grind dried curry leaves with roasted lentils, chilies, and salt. Store as a dry chutney powder to sprinkle over rice with ghee.

  4. 4. Curry Leaf Tea

    Steep 15-20 dried curry leaves in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain and add lemon/honey. Drink on an empty stomach for digestive benefits.

  5. 5. Chutneys & Dips

    Add crushed dried curry leaves to coconut chutney, tomato chutney, or mint chutney for extra depth and aroma.

  6. 6. Sambar & Rasam

    Essential in South Indian soups. Add to the tempering (tadka) at the start for authentic flavor in sambar and rasam.

  7. 7. Vegetable Stir-Fries

    Fry dried curry leaves with spices at the start, then add vegetables. Works great with okra, beans, or cabbage.

  8. 8. Infused Oil

    Fry dried curry leaves in coconut or sesame oil until crisp. Strain and use the infused oil for cooking or as a finishing touch.

  9. 9. Garnish for Soups & Stews

    Crush dried curry leaves and sprinkle over finished soups, stews, or dals as a flavorful garnish.

  10. 10. Fusion Dishes

    Add to pasta, roasted vegetables, or even scrambled eggs for an Indian twist. Start with a small amount and adjust to taste.
