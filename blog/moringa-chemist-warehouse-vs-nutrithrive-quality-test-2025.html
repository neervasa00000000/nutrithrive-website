What you typically get at Chemist Warehouse

Australian pharmacy chains often list moringa as capsules or tablets from third-party supplement brands. Stock varies by location and season. Pure moringa leaf powder is less common on those shelves than capsules. When you pick up a pack, the useful comparison points are: milligrams per capsule, number of “other ingredients,” country of origin, and whether the brand publishes any testing or batch information.

At-home “quality test” (what you can actually check)

We are not substituting a lab here—these are practical checks any buyer can use before or after purchase:

  • Label honesty: 100% moringa leaf, or a blend? Blends are not a fair comparison to pure powder.
  • Dose math: If a cap is 500 mg, how many caps equal a level teaspoon (~2–3 g) of powder? That drives true daily cost.
  • Colour and smell (powder): Should be vibrant green and earthy—not dull brown or musty.
  • Transparency: Can you find a CoA or batch discussion, or only marketing copy?
  • Freshness habits: Tight storage, no clear “packed on” date? Treat shelf time as a risk for any retailer.
