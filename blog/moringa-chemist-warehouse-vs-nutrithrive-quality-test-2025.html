⚠️ Rosabella Moringa Recall (February 2026): What You Need to Know

In February 2026, Rosabella issued a voluntary recall of multiple moringa capsule batches due to potential Salmonella contamination — specifically an extensively drug-resistant strain. This was reported to the relevant food safety authorities.

What happened:

  • Date: February 2026
  • Reason: Potential contamination with Salmonella (drug-resistant strain)
  • Affected: Multiple lot numbers — check the FDA/TGA recall database to verify your bottle
  • Symptoms of concern: Fever, diarrhoea, abdominal cramping

What to do if you bought Rosabella moringa recently:

  1. Check the lot number on your bottle against official recall lists (FDA recall database or TGA)
  2. If your batch is affected, do not consume — return to Chemist Warehouse for a refund
  3. Contact your doctor if you have experienced symptoms

This recall raises serious questions about quality control — and it's exactly why we decided to run our own quality tests.

How to Check Rosabella Moringa Quality Yourself

Whether you already have a bottle or are considering buying one, these checks take under two minutes:

  1. Open a capsule: Break one open and look at the powder. Vibrant green = relatively fresh. Brown or dull olive = oxidized. Rosabella's clear bottle gives UV light direct access to the powder from the moment it hits the shelf.
  2. Smell it: Fresh moringa smells earthy and slightly grassy. Musty, flat, or stale smells mean degradation has already started.
  3. Check the lot number: Cross-reference against the February 2026 recall notice on the FDA recall database. Our batch code guide explains what to look for.
  4. Do the dose maths: At 800mg per serving, how many capsules do you need per day to reach 2,000mg? How long will your bottle last at that rate? Recalculate the true daily cost.
  5. Ask for lab results: A brand confident in their product will share a Certificate of Analysis on request. If they won't, that tells you something.
