How to Check Moringa Quality: 5 Tests Anyone Can Do in 2 Minutes

Whether you already have a bottle or are considering buying one, these checks take under two minutes:

  1. Open a capsule: Break one open and look at the powder. Vibrant green = relatively fresh. Brown or dull olive = oxidized. Rosabella's clear bottle gives UV light direct access to the powder from the moment it hits the shelf.
  2. Smell it: Fresh moringa smells earthy and slightly grassy. Musty, flat, or stale smells mean degradation has already started.
  3. Check the lot number: Cross-reference against the February 2026 recall notice on the FDA recall database. Our batch code guide explains what to look for.
  4. Do the dose maths: At 800mg per serving, how many capsules do you need per day to reach 2,000mg? How long will your bottle last at that rate? Recalculate the true daily cost.
  5. Ask for lab results: A brand confident in their product will share a Certificate of Analysis on request. If they won't, that tells you something.
