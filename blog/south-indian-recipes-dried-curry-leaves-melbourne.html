üçã 1. Lemon Rice (Chitranna)

Keyword focus: lemon rice with curry leaves, South Indian rice recipe Melbourne

Lemon rice is a zesty, comforting South Indian dish made with cooked rice, lemon juice, turmeric, and a tempering of spices and curry leaves.

How to make it:

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan.
  2. Add mustard seeds, urad dal, dried red chillies, and a generous handful of dried curry leaves.
  3. Once the tempering splutters, stir in turmeric and cooked rice.
  4. Add lemon juice and salt, toss well, and serve warm.

üí° Pro tip: Use about 2‚Äì3√ó more dried curry leaves than fresh to match the intensity. The dried leaves crisp up beautifully in the hot oil.

üçÖ 2. Rasam (South Indian Spiced Broth)

Keyword focus: rasam with dried curry leaves, South Indian soup recipe

Rasam is a tangy tomato-based broth flavoured with tamarind, spices, and ‚Äî most importantly ‚Äî a curry leaf tadka.

How to make it:

  1. Simmer tomatoes, tamarind water, rasam powder, and salt until fragrant.
  2. Separately, temper mustard seeds, cumin, black pepper, garlic, and dried curry leaves in ghee.
  3. Pour the tempering into the simmering rasam and garnish with coriander.

How to make it:

  2. Separately, heat ghee and fry garlic, cumin, mustard seeds, dried red chillies, and dried curry leaves until aromatic.
  3. Pour this sizzling tadka over the cooked dal and mix.

This is a classic South Indian dry chutney powder made from curry leaves, dals, and spices.

How to make it:

  1. Dry roast dried curry leaves, urad dal, chana dal, black pepper, cumin, and red chillies.
  2. Grind into a coarse powder with salt.
  3. Mix the powder with hot rice and ghee.
