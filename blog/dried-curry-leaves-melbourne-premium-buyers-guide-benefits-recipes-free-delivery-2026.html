10 Everyday Ways to Use Dried Curry Leaves

1) Dal (Lentil) Tadka

Temper ghee with mustard, cumin, dried leaves; pour over dal.

2) Lemon Rice

Peanuts, turmeric, dried leaves; fold into steamed rice.

3) Coconut Chutney

Fry dried leaves; pour over coconut‑chilli chutney.

4) Rasam

Tamarind‑tomato broth; finish with dried leaves.

5) Poha / Upma

Temper onions with dried leaves and peanuts.

6) Karuveppilai Podi

Roast dried leaves + dals + chilli; grind coarse.

7) Snack Seasoning

Crush and toss with roasted nuts or popcorn.

8) Curry‑Leaf Tea

Simmer 10–15 leaves 5–7 mins; sip after meals.

9) Marinades

Add crushed leaves to yogurt‑spice rubs.

10) Infused Oil

Warm coconut oil with leaves; drizzle over bowls.

HowTo: Curry Leaf Tea (Digestive & Detox Tonic)

  1. Bring 1 cup (250 ml) water to a gentle boil.
  2. Add 12 dried curry leaves; simmer 5–7 minutes.
  3. Strain; add lemon or honey if desired. Enjoy warm after meals.

Tip: Pair with a teaspoon of Moringa Powder in a smoothie for an energising start. Learn about curry leaves health benefits and how to cook with dried curry leaves.

HowTo: Curry Leaf Powder (Karuveppilai Podi)

  • Roast 20 g dried leaves until crisp and fragrant.
  • Separately roast 50 g chana dal + 50 g urad dal until golden.
  • Combine with 2 tbsp chilli flakes, a pinch of asafoetida, salt.
  • Cool completely; grind coarse. Store airtight. Serve with rice + ghee.

Storage & Shelf Life Tips (Melbourne)

  • Store in an airtight container, cool dark cupboard.
  • Avoid fridge unless necessary (humidity can dull aroma).
  • Replace after 8–12 months for maximum flavour.
  • Crush a leaf; if aroma is faint, it’s time to restock.
