What makes Darjeeling black tea special?

Unlike the robust Assam variety used in most Indian black teas, Darjeeling uses the Chinese cultivar Camellia sinensis var. sinensis, which has smaller leaves and a more delicate aroma. Insects such as leafhoppers and thrips nibble on the leaves, prompting the plants to produce aromatic monoterpenes (especially linalool) and C13‚Äënorisoprenoids that create the signature muscatel flavour.
