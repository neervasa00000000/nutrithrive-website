The "Internal Shower" is Broken. Here is Why.

To understand why we need an upgrade, we must first understand the mechanics of the chia trend. The "Internal Shower" works primarily on a mechanical level. Chia seeds are rich in soluble fiber and mucilage. When soaked, they absorb up to 12 times their weight in water, forming a gel that adds bulk to the stool and aids motility.

However, this mechanical action comes with significant downsides:

The Bloat Paradox: For many, the sudden influx of heavy, gel-forming fiber can overwhelm the digestive tract, trapping gas and causing severe distention‚Äîthe exact opposite of the desired effect.

Safety Risks: Medical reports have highlighted cases where dry or semi-dry chia seeds have expanded in the esophagus, creating blockages that require medical intervention.

Nutrient Limitations: While chia is an excellent source of Omega-3s and fiber, it lacks the broad-spectrum micronutrients required to support the energy systems of the body. You might be "flushed," but you are still tired.
