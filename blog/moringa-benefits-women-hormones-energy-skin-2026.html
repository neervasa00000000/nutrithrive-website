Why Women Need Different Nutrition Than Men

Most supplement advice is written for men, by men. Cool. But I don't need more testosterone.

1. More iron

Women lose blood every month. Result: about 1 in 3 women are iron deficient — exhaustion, pale skin, hair loss, brain fog, always cold. Most iron tablets cause constipation. Moringa is very iron-dense plus vitamin C for absorption (often described as having far more iron per gram than spinach — check your product label).

2. Hormone balance (not more hormones)

Women's hormones shift weekly and through life stages. Moringa doesn't add synthetic hormones like the pill — it supports your body with B6, magnesium, antioxidants, and adaptogenic compounds that help stress resilience. See gut and skin healing for how internal balance shows on your face.

3. Bone density (especially after 30)

When estrogen drops in perimenopause and menopause, bones weaken faster — osteoporosis risk is much higher in women. Moringa provides calcium, vitamin K, magnesium, and protein for bone structure.

4. Skin from the inside

Hormonal acne, inflammation, and nutrient gaps drive most "skincare failures." Moringa's antioxidants, vitamin A, and anti-inflammatory compounds support skin alongside gut health.

Safety & Side Effects (Honest Version)

Moringa has been eaten as food for thousands of years. Who should be careful:

  • Pregnant: leaves/powder only with obstetrician approval — never root or bark
  • Thyroid medication: may affect thyroid — ask your doctor
  • Blood thinners (warfarin): vitamin K content — medical review needed
  • Diabetes medication: may lower blood sugar — monitor levels

Common mild side effects: digestive upset or nausea if you start too high or take on an empty stomach. Start low, take with food, increase slowly.
