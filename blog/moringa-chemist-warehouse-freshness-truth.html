Why Freshness Matters: The Science Behind Dead vs. Alive Moringa

Moringa's power comes from its chlorophyll (the green pigment that means it's fresh) and antioxidants (the compounds that fight free radicals). Both degrade rapidly when exposed to:

  • Heat: Industrial heat-drying at high temperatures kills 40-60% of nutrients. Most mass-market brands use this method because it's fast and cheap.
  • Light: Fluorescent store lighting breaks down chlorophyll over time. That's why old Moringa looks brown ‚Äî the chlorophyll is gone.
  • Time: After 6 months, antioxidant levels drop by 70%. After 12 months? You're left with fiber and dust.
  • Oxygen: Poor packaging (clear bags, loose seals) lets air in, oxidizing nutrients. The powder goes stale faster.
