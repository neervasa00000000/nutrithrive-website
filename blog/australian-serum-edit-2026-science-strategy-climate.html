7Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the main difference between Retinol and Retinaldehyde?

Retinaldehyde acts up to 11 times faster than Retinol because it only requires one conversion step within the skin to become active.

2. Which serum ingredient is best for high-humidity climates like Queensland?

Niacinamide is the top choice for humidity as it regulates oil production and prevents the "sticky" feeling caused by heavy products.

3. Why is Kakadu Plum considered superior to standard Vitamin C?

It is the world's richest natural source of Vitamin C (up to 5,300mg per 100g) and contains additional co-factors that improve absorption and stability.

4. How does the "Nutri Thrive" brand differ from "Nutra Thrive"?

Nutri Thrive is a holistic human wellness and skincare brand, while Nutra Thrive is a pet supplement brand featuring bacon-flavored powders.

5. What is the best skincare strategy for Melbourne's variable weather?

You must prioritize occlusion by layering a Hyaluronic Acid serum under a lipid-rich moisturizer to seal the barrier against cold winds and low humidity.

6. Is it safe to use Vitamin C and Retinol at the same time?

It is best to separate them‚Äîusing Vitamin C in the morning for protection and Retinol at night for repair‚Äîto avoid pH conflicts and irritation.

7. Which active ingredient is recommended for pregnancy-safe anti-aging?

Bakuchiol is the ideal alternative as it mimics Retinol's collagen-stimulating effects without the risks associated with Vitamin A derivatives.

8. Why is SkinCeuticals C E Ferulic so expensive compared to other serums?

It utilizes a patented stabilization method that combines L-Ascorbic Acid with Ferulic Acid at a precise pH (<3.5) for guaranteed clinical efficacy.

9. What function does Tasmanian Pepperberry serve in skincare formulations?

It contains Polygodial, a potent anti-inflammatory agent that instantly reduces itching and burning sensations in sensitive skin.

10. What is the purpose of "Cellular Extraction" in Australian Native products?

It uses high pressure and low temperature to extract plant compounds without heat, preserving the ingredient's true-to-nature profile and potency.
