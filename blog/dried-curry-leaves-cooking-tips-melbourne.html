The Myth: "Dried Leaves Have No Flavor"

Unlike fresh leaves, dried leaves need to be woken up. The essential oils are dormant, locked inside the dehydrated leaf. You have to unlock them.

How to Use Dried Curry Leaves in Soup Australia (The Secret Trick)

Want to know how to use dried curry leaves in soup? The secret is the "Hot Oil" Infusion (Tadka). To make dried curry leaves taste 90% as good as fresh, you must follow this one rule:

Tadka Recipe for Beginners: Always fry them in hot fat

  1. Heat your ghee, coconut oil, or vegetable oil until shimmering.
  2. Crush the dried leaves slightly in your hand to break the cell walls.
  3. Throw them into the hot oil before you add onions or garlic.
  4. Wait 10 seconds. You will smell a sudden burst of citrus and nuttiness. That is the flavor releasing.

Adding Curry Leaves to Tea Australia: Melbourne Tea Recipe

Want to try adding curry leaves to tea? It's a traditional Ayurvedic practice that's gaining popularity in Australia. Here's how:

Curry Leaf Tea Recipe:

  1. Boil 1 cup of water
  2. Add 10-15 dried curry leaves
  3. Simmer for 5-7 minutes
  4. Strain and add lemon or honey (optional)
  5. Drink warm after meals for digestive benefits
