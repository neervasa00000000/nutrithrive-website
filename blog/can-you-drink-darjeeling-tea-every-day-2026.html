Can You Drink Darjeeling Tea Every Day? Benefits and Limits (2026)

By Neer, NutriThrive Truganina · Last updated: 29 Jun 2026

Daily tea drinking is one of the most consistent habits across the longest-lived and healthiest populations studied. Darjeeling specifically — yes, drinking it every day is generally fine for most adults, with a few things worth knowing.

The case for daily Darjeeling

Regular black tea consumption is associated with real, documented benefits. The polyphenols in black tea — theaflavins and thearubigins — are studied for antioxidant activity, gut microbiome diversity support, and cardiovascular effects. Drinking black tea daily (1-3 cups) is consistently associated with better health outcomes in observational studies — whether causally or as a marker of other healthy habits is hard to fully disentangle, but the direction is consistent.

Darjeeling’s high-altitude growing conditions and lighter processing give it a broader polyphenol profile than many heavily processed commercial blends, so the quality of what you’re drinking matters — but daily tea drinking in general is a positive dietary habit.

The caffeine ceiling

Darjeeling contains roughly 40-50mg caffeine per cup. The general safe upper limit for healthy adults is around 400mg per day. At 1-3 cups, you’re at 40-150mg — comfortably within range. If you’re also drinking coffee or other caffeinated drinks, that’s where total caffeine adds up. Pregnant women are advised to keep total caffeine below 200mg per day.

The iron absorption issue

This is the main daily-drinking consideration that’s genuinely worth knowing. Tannins in all black tea — Darjeeling included — reduce non-haem iron absorption when tea is consumed with or immediately after meals. If you’re monitoring iron intake (which matters for many women, vegetarians, and people with low iron), drinking Darjeeling with meals consistently could work against your iron goals.

The practical fix: leave a 30-60 minute gap between meals and your Darjeeling. Drink it mid-morning or mid-afternoon rather than immediately with breakfast or lunch.

Who should be more careful

Pregnant women: stick to 1-2 cups maximum given caffeine guidance for pregnancy. People with diagnosed iron deficiency: pay attention to timing relative to meals. People with anxiety or caffeine sensitivity: start with one cup and see how you respond. Anyone on medication: flagging regular tea consumption to your GP is worth doing, since tannins can affect how some medications are absorbed.

FAQ

Is daily Darjeeling tea safe?

Yes, for most healthy adults. 1-3 cups is a reasonable daily range.

How many cups per day?

1-3 cups is the supported healthy range.

Does it affect iron absorption?

Yes. Leave 30-60 minutes gap from iron-rich meals.
