The Dermatologist Said My Acne Was Severe — and Discussed Prescription Options

I was 23. My face was covered in painful cystic acne.

Not cute little whiteheads. Deep, angry, under-the-skin cysts that hurt to touch. The kind that left scars. The kind that made strangers stare.

I'd tried everything: $200 dermatologist appointments (7 doctors), $85/month prescription creams, $60 Instagram "miracle" serums, birth control (made it worse), antibiotics (worked 2 months, then stopped).

Nothing worked long-term. The dermatologist discussed isotretinoin (a prescription medicine with serious risks and monitoring). I wanted to try lifestyle and gut work first — that was my choice, not medical advice for anyone else.

I spent months learning what might be driving my breakouts. For me, gut inflammation and diet triggers seemed to matter as much as topicals.

Over 12+ weeks of diet changes plus moringa as a food supplement, my skin looked noticeably better — not perfect for everyone. This is what I did (and what didn't).

Is This You? (Take the Test)

You likely have hormonal / gut-driven acne if:

  • Breakouts around jawline, chin, or neck
  • Deep, painful cysts (not surface whiteheads)
  • Acne flares 1 week before period
  • Started in your 20s (not teen years)
  • Topicals don't work (benzoyl peroxide, salicylic acid)
  • Stress makes it worse
  • Dairy = instant breakout
  • You also have bloating, irregular periods, or bad PMS

4+ checked: keep reading. 7+: this guide is for you.

Why Your Acne Won't Clear (What Dermatologists Don't Tell You)

Dermatologists say: bacteria + oil + clogged pores → use this cream. That's symptom management — like mopping while the sink overflows.

Hormonal acne = internal problem

  1. Gut is broken (leaky gut, bad bacteria)
  2. Systemic inflammation
  3. Inflammation triggers hormones (androgens, cortisol)
  4. More sebum + inflammation = cystic acne

For some people, improving gut health may help lower inflammation that affects skin — but this is not a guarantee, and severe acne still needs a dermatologist.

FAQs

Will this work for cystic acne?

I had 15–20 active cysts. For me, weeks 1–4 were slow (gut adjustment), 5–8 showed fewer new breakouts, 9–12 was when I saw the biggest change. Be patient — and stay in touch with a dermatologist if acne is severe.

Do I really need to cut dairy?

For most hormonal acne: yes. Cut 30 days strict, reintroduce 1 serving, watch 48–72 hours. If you break out, it's a trigger.

Probiotics alone without diet?

No. Sugar, dairy, and processed food undo probiotics. Remove triggers + add healers (probiotics, moringa, real food).

How long until results?

Weeks 2–3: digestion · 4–6: fewer new breakouts for me · 8–12: gradual healing · months 4–6: maintenance. Weeks 1–4 may "purge" for some — see a dermatologist if it worsens.

Why moringa for acne?

Anti-inflammatory (quercetin, chlorogenic acid), prebiotic fiber, vitamin A for skin turnover, zinc for oil control — ~$11/month. NutriThrive Moringa

What about topicals?

Use simple support only: AM water rinse + moisturizer + SPF; PM gentle cleanser + retinol 2×/week + moisturizer. 80% internal, 20% topical.

Will acne come back?

If you return to dairy, sugar, no probiotics — yes. Maintenance: 80/20 diet, daily moringa + probiotic, stress and sleep. Lifestyle, not a quick fix.
