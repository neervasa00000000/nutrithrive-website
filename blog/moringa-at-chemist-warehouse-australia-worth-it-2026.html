The problem with buying moringa from big retailers

Most people assume bigger stores = better products. With moringa, quality and freshness matter more than branding.

1) Limited strength options

Many retail capsules contain small doses, meaning you need to take multiple pills daily to reach a meaningful serving size.

2) Older stock

Plant powders can lose their “fresh” edge over time. If a product sits longer on shelves, you may get a less vibrant powder (or capsules filled from older powder).

3) Lack of transparency

It’s often unclear where the moringa is sourced from, how it’s processed, and whether it’s truly pure.

What makes high-quality moringa?

If you want real results, use this checklist when you buy:

  • 100% pure moringa (no fillers)
  • Bright green colour (a practical freshness signal)
  • Fine texture (easy to mix)
  • Clear sourcing (where it comes from, how it’s processed)

Avoid anything that looks dull or brownish, lists unnecessary additives, or doesn’t clearly explain origin and handling.
