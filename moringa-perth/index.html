Storing moringa in Perth's extreme summer heat

Perth is one of the hottest major cities in Australia, with temperatures frequently exceeding 40°C in January and February; heatwaves above 45°C are not unusual. This is a significant challenge for moringa storage, as heat accelerates oxidation and colour loss more rapidly than in cooler climates.

  • Keep the sealed pouch in your coolest, darkest cupboard, not above the stove or near the dishwasher.
  • After opening, press excess air out, reseal tightly, and refrigerate throughout Perth summer (November–March).
  • Allow the bag to return to room temperature before opening to avoid condensation inside the pack.
  • Never leave moringa in a car boot or garage during Perth summer; temperatures inside can exceed 60°C.
  • If you notice the colour fading from vivid green to olive, the powder has begun oxidising; use it sooner rather than later.
